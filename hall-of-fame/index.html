Responsible Disclosure Policy

We welcome security researchers and bug hunters to help make our application more secure. We are an open security community and do not offer monetary bounties, but we do recognize and reward contributors with:

Recognition and Rewards

  • An official Appreciation Letter you can add to your resume.
  • A permanent listing on our Heroes Wall (option to remain publicly anonymous if you prefer).
  • Social recognition on our channels (with your consent).

Scope — What you’re invited to test

(Tests are allowed only within the scope below and under the Safe Testing Rules.)

In Scope:

  • https://cracksoftsecurity.github.io/
  • Any subdomains or apps explicitly listed on this site

Out of Scope:

  • Internal-only/admin-only networks or services unless explicitly authorized.
  • Third-party services (payment processors, analytics, CDNs) that you do not have explicit permission to test.
  • Social engineering, phishing, or any attempts to access other users’ accounts by guessing or brute force without prior written permission.

Safe Testing Rules (Must Follow)

  • Do not exploit vulnerabilities for personal gain or to damage data/systems.
  • Test only the assets explicitly listed as in scope.
  • Do not attempt to access, modify, or exfiltrate real user data. If you discover user data, stop and report immediately.
  • Do not perform destructive tests (mass deletion, ransomware-style encryption, DDOS/traffic flooding).
  • Do not publicly disclose vulnerabilities until they are fixed or you have explicit permission from us.
  • Use only test accounts or accounts you own. Do not attempt privilege escalation into other users’ accounts.
  • Keep a clear, reproducible proof-of-concept (POC) that demonstrates the vulnerability without leaking private data.
  • Avoid automated scraping or crawling that can affect system performance. Notify us first if you plan heavy automated testing.

Report & POC Requirements

Report Template (Plain Text)

  • Subject: [Responsible Disclosure] Short title — affected asset
  • Title: Short, clear vulnerability title
  • Affected asset(s): exact URL(s) / app version / endpoint / IPs
  • Severity: (e.g., Low / Medium / High / Critical) and brief rationale
  • Impact: What an attacker could do (data exposure, account takeover, RCE, etc.)
  • Steps to reproduce: numbered steps that we can follow exactly (include requests, parameters, headers, example payloads)
  • Proof of concept: short explanation plus timestamps/logs
  • Mitigation suggestion: what fix you recommend
  • Disclosure preference: public name or anonymous, permission to share on our social handles, or remain private

POC Video Guidance

  • Keep it short and focused (1–3 minutes recommended).
  • Show only the steps needed to reproduce — do not display or exfiltrate real user data.
  • Narrate or caption the steps, include timestamps and target URLs.
  • Export as mp4 or share a secure link (Drive/Dropbox) with viewing permissions.

Response & Triage Timeline

We will acknowledge receipt of your report by email (within our operational capacity).

1. Acknowledgement: We confirm receipt and assign a tracking number.

2. Triage: We review and classify severity; we may ask for clarification.

3. Fix: We prioritize and fix based on severity and impact.

4. Close: After fix verification, we close the issue and issue recognition.

Severity Guidance (Example)

  • Low: Minor info leak, UI issues, missing security headers.
  • Medium: Authentication logic flaws with limited impact, insufficient access control in non-sensitive areas.
  • High: Sensitive data exposure, account takeover vectors, injection that can modify data.
  • Critical: Remote code execution, full database access, or other flaws that can cause irreversible damage or mass data breach.

We may adjust severity after internal verification.

Prohibited Actions & Consequences

We take abuse very seriously. If you intentionally misuse any discovered vulnerability or attempt to harm our users or systems, we will take appropriate action. This is a non-exhaustive list of actions that may lead to consequences:

  • Accessing, modifying, deleting, or exfiltrating real user data.
  • Performing distributed denial-of-service (DDoS) attacks or flooding services.
  • Deploying or running malware, ransomware, or persistence mechanisms.
  • Selling access to our systems, data, or vulnerabilities to third parties.
  • Social engineering of our staff, users, or contractors.
  • Publicly disclosing unpatched vulnerabilities (full technical details) before we have fixed them or given permission.

Possible consequences include immediate revocation of access, permanent exclusion from our community, and referral to law enforcement where required.

Legal Safe Harbor

We welcome security research in good faith. If you follow this policy and act in good faith to avoid privacy invasion, data theft, and system harm, we will not pursue civil action against you for those good-faith actions.
